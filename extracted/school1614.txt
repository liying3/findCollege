What the School Says...
Old Dominion University in Norfolk, Virginia, is built for students who thrive in the modern world, who see possibility around every corner, and who know that knowledge and experience are the roads to success. With more than 160 mind-expanding programs, including 69 undergraduate degrees and internships in every imaginable field, you can bring your thoughts to life at Old Dominion.
What the School Says...
Freshman students should make sure to apply for admissions by December 1 for priority merit-based scholarship consideration or meet our regular deadline of February 1. Transfer students have various deadlines depending on their intended semester of entry. Old Dominion requires freshman applicants to submit their official SAT scores for critical reading and mathematics or their ACT scores. Test scores must be sent directly from the testing agency or included on the official high school transcript. Transfer students who graduated from high school less than two years prior to their application must submit official SAT or ACT scores.
What the School Says...
Over half of students attending Old Dominion get financial help from one source or another, including federal and state grants, scholarships, educational loans, or through college work-study programs. Last year, over 80 percent of students who applied for financial aid received assistance.
What the School Says...
At Old Dominion, we get it. Inspiration comes from the most unexpected places. And thought doesn't always present itself in words but in pictures, sounds, numbers, or notes. Our classes are built to unleash the power of thought in many forms. Old Dominion faculty and students are shaping the way that we interact with our environment, give voice to our reason, and live in the coming years.
What the School Says...
The best word to describe Old Dominion's housing options is variety. Whether they choose to live in apartment-style housing in University Village, or in one of our traditional residence halls, students residing on campus experience university life to its fullest. Students' interests are reflected in the more than 250 social, professional, religious, academic, and recreational clubs and organizations on campus. 
What the School Says...
With 24,000 students from more than 130 different countries and across the U.S., our global community is a rich and welcoming mix of nationalities, cultures, viewpoints, and life experiences. From extensive study abroad programs to our award-winning Career Management Center, from internships and co-ops in every field imaginable to hundreds of volunteer and outreach initiatives, you’ll gain real-world skills and exciting experiences outside the classroom.
