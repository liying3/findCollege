What the School Says...
Pratt Institute is one of the leading art, design, and architecture schools in the United States. Founded in 1887 and located on a 25-acre, beautifully landscaped, enclosed campus in Brooklyn, N.Y., Pratt provides students with an outstanding professional education and a traditional residential college experience within the exciting, creative environment of a top art school.
What the School Says...
Pratt's application deadline is January 5 for regular decision, November 1 for early action, and February 1 for transfer applicants. Our spring deadline is October 1. The undergraduate and graduate admissions committees base their decisions on a careful review of all credentials submitted by the applicant, including transcripts, test scores, and portfolio submissions if required. We urge you to complete and file your application by the stated deadlines in order to be given full consideration. 
What the School Says...
Pratt offers various types of financial assistance, including tuition scholarships and grants, loans, student employment, and tuition management programs.  By combining federal, state, and institutional funds we make every effort to assist you in meeting the increasing cost of a college education. Our financial aid process is simple and straightforward. Students are required to fill out the FAFSA for need-based aid.  All accepted students are automatically considered for merit-based scholarships.  
What the School Says...
Pratt offers 22 undergraduate degree programs in art, design, architecture, and writing. Many of these programs are nationally ranked among the top ten. Pratt also offers 25 graduate degree programs in its schools of Art and Design, Architecture, Liberal Arts and Sciences, and Information and Library Science. The faculty includes award-winning artists, architects, designers, and writers who connect students with internships and often jobs. In addition to undergraduate and graduate degrees, Pratt offers two Associate's Degree programs: a career-oriented AOS track, which prepares students for careers in the art and design professions, and a transfer-oriented AAS track, which positions graduates to transfer directly into the junior year of the Pratt BFA programs in Fine Arts or Communication Design.  
What the School Says...
Pratt has two campuses and two affiliate campuses. The main campus is a beautifully landscaped, 25-acre, tree-lined area in a quiet residential neighborhood in Brooklyn, N.Y., 25 minutes from Manhattan. Five residence halls for graduates and undergraduates offer students an environment that promotes learning and development. Exhibitions, films, lecture series, and student clubs are offered during evenings and weekends. Pratt's contemporary sculpture park, ranked one of the top-ten college sculpture exhibitions, provides students with a firsthand look at the work of today's important sculptors. Pratt's Manhattan campus is located in an historic, renovated building in Chelsea, N.Y. Pratt also has two affiliate campuses, Pratt MWP, located in Utica, N.Y., and DCAD, located in Delaware.  
What the School Says...
Pratt Institute attracts highly motivated, talented students from a variety of backgrounds. In fact, almost 20 percent of the freshman class is international. Pratt's student population represents students from over 80 different countries. U.S. students come from just about every state, with 27 percent from New York. A Pratt degree will open doors throughout your life, and Pratt graduates head into design and architecture firms throughout the country and the world. Eighty-four percent of graduates are employed six months after graduation and 95 percent are employed in their field. You will be able to use our online career services to find work throughout your career, and feel confident that you too will make important contributions and change our world for the better.
