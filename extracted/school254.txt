What the School Says...
America's sixth-oldest college, Moravian College is a private, selective, liberal arts college located in Bethlehem, Penn., in the heart of the Lehigh Valley. A thriving metropolitan area of more than 700,000 people, the Lehigh Valley blends a sense of history with a vision for the future. The campus setting is suburban, approximately 90 miles west of New York City and 60 miles north of Philadelphia. 
What the School Says...
We expect our students to be academically engaged and active in promoting on-campus activities. We look for these same qualities among our applicants. During the application process, your admission counselor will closely and comprehensively review your application. We base our admissions decisions on the totality of your high school career. We closely assess your transcript, including course selection and performance in those classes, extracurricular activities, leadership skills, community activism, and recommendations. Standardized test scores are required, but we place greater emphasis on your entire application, especially your transcript. The admission staff also encourages a campus visit. We believe that getting to know prospective students individually only adds to our personal approach in the selection process.
What the School Says...
Recently, over 90 percent of freshmen received scholarships and grants totaling over $5.6 million. The average scholarship or grant awarded to incoming freshmen was around $15,000. Eighty-four percent of freshmen received loans, with an average need-based loan of around $4,000. To be considered for financial aid, students should complete the FAFSA and the Moravian College Financial Aid Application.
What the School Says...
The college offers bachelor's degrees (the Bachelor of Arts, Bachelor of Science, and Bachelor of Music) in 32 major fields and more than 50 programs of study. Our general education "Learning in Common" program includes multidisciplinary courses designed to sharpen critical skills such as writing, critical thinking, ethical reasoning, knowledge of economic and social systems, and quantitative reasoning. The curriculum provides students with the skills they will need to think, to interpret the world, and to compete in the economic and cultural climate of the new century. Together, those who teach here and those who learn here form a community that includes a scholarly, caring faculty, dedicated to teaching undergraduates. 
What the School Says...
Moravian students are actively involved in the life of the college. Students participate in student government, sing with our world-renowned college choir, play in intramural and varsity sports, help direct the student-managed Amrhein Investment Fund, take active part in professional societies related to their studies, and make lasting friendships with other students, faculty, staff, and graduates of the college.
What the School Says...
Over the last ten years, we have focused on enhancing every aspect of Moravian that affects the quality of the student experience, from our engaged faculty, to new and technologically up-to-date academic and athletics facilities, to updated student services.  We seek to develop in students of all backgrounds the capacity to learn, reflect, reason, communicate, and act with integrity as individuals and in association with others.  This education prepares men and women for advanced study, continuous learning, individual achievement, and leadership and service for the common good.
