What the School Says...
With personal attention and a professional focus, Western New England University in Springfield, Mass., prepares students for successful careers or graduate school through its Colleges of Arts and Sciences, Business, Engineering, Law, and Pharmacy. Students find us centrally located for weekend visits to New York City, Boston, or the beaches and mountains of New England.
What the School Says...
We have a "rolling" admissions policy, not a set admissions deadline, which gives students greater flexibility in applying. However, the University encourages students to apply by the priority application date of February 15. We require SAT or ACT test scores. We do not require any SAT Subject Tests. International students can substitute TOEFL scores in place of SAT or ACT scores.
What the School Says...
About 90 percent of Western New England University students receive some form of financial aid. Assistance may include merit scholarships, need-based scholarships, grants, employment, and loans. Merit based scholarships range from $5,500 to $16,000.
What the School Says...
You can choose from over 40 majors and programs and add minors from your own school or across disciplines. From pre-pharmacy to forensic chemistry, biomedical engineering to computer science, accounting to sport management, Western New England University offers exciting majors that lead to jobs in some of the fastest-growing fields in the world. We offer accelerated degrees in business, engineering, and law, including the only Biomedical Engineering/Law program in the country.
What the School Says...
As you move closer to graduation and life in the working world, your living options at Western New England University change. Freshmen live in one of five residence halls, co-ed by floor, with double-occupancy rooms, lounges, kitchenettes, laundry facilities, data-ports, broadband service, and cable TV. Options for upperclassmen include suites, apartments, or townhouses. 
What the School Says...
In class you may learn about marketing, teaching, or engineering. But what is it really like to work in those fields or others on a day-to-day basis? When you participate in an internship or field experience, you'll work side-by-side with professionals and learn about future career paths. Western New England University works with a network of over 1,500 businesses and organizations where students can do field work, conduct engineering design projects, teach, or participate in internships.
