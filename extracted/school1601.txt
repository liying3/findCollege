What the School Says...
Life at University of North Carolina at Charlotte is full of possibilities. We are a research university made up of seven colleges offering 87 programs leading to a bachelor's degree. Our vibrant, exciting, 1,000-acre campus is nestled into one of America's fastest-growing cities, and our future is as bright as our students. The City of Charlotte, recently rated the number-one city in which to live, provides ample opportunities for entertainment, the arts, and career exploration.  This year, more students chose UNC Charlotte for their undergraduate education than ever before.
What the School Says...
Admissions decisions are based on your weighted GPA, SAT or ACT scores, course selection, and extracurricular activities.  Students who submit complete applications by November 1 are guaranteed a decision by January 30. For more information about our deadlines and notifications, visit our admissions website. We will combine the best scores from each SAT or ACT test section (critical reading, math, and writing) when making an admissions decision. Students taking the ACT are required to take the writing section. Students should also send their official AP, IB, and CLEP exam scores to the admissions office for evaluation of credit.
What the School Says...
UNC Charlotte's cost of tuition, room, and board is below the national average for four-year public schools. Scholarships and other forms of financial aid are available. Financial aid applications should be complete and ready for review by March 1.
What the School Says...
Students may choose their field of study from a wide range of majors in these seven academic colleges: Arts and Architecture, Liberal Arts and Sciences, Business, Computing and Informatics, Education, Engineering, and Health and Human Services. Students benefit not only from an extensive focus on practical, hands-on experience but also accessibility to state-of-the-art facilities.  Ninety percent of our students take part in professional internships or research opportunities to gain valuable real-world experience. UNC Charlotte is the only university to have on its faculty three Carnegie Foundation Professors of the Year. 
What the School Says...
Approximately 5,000 students live on campus at UNC Charlotte. There are more than 350 campus organizations and programs available, ranging from fraternities and sororities to religious organizations to intramural sports. This is a great time to be exploring UNC Charlotte. In the fall of 2013, we will celebrate the inaugural season of our 49er football team, and all our sports teams will be joining Conference USA.  We invite you to visit campus to discover all that UNC Charlotte and the city of Charlotte have to offer. We look forward to showcasing our quality academic programs, modern campus facilities, and active student life. 
What the School Says...
The UNC Charlotte campus is like a small, thriving city. It is full of dynamic, creative and diverse people who are immersed in planning projects, starting conversations, sharing ideas, and making things happen. You'll never be bored here as long as you get involved, because everywhere you look, something amazing is happening. Whether you are participating yourself, checking out the work of your classmates, or being entertained by a musician, there's always something beautiful happening on campus. Students have the opportunity to gain a well-rounded educational experience by engaging in fulfilling activities such as athletic events, musicals, writing for Niner Media, building a Habitat for Humanity house, and much more.
