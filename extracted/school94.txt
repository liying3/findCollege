What the School Says...
Welcome to the oldest and most distinguished institution in the United States that focuses on the study of the environment. At SUNY College of Environmental Science and Forestry, you will study with professors whose work improves and sustains the environment from the Yucatan to Alaska, and whose expertise is sought by governments and corporations worldwide. That same faculty will be personally concerned with your progress. Their cutting-edge research will become part of your SUNY-ESF educational experience, and that experience will merge with the world beyond the college. Do you intend to make your living by helping to shape our environmental future? If the answer is yes, SUNY-ESF can help you reach your goals.
What the School Says...
Successful freshman applicants should present strong high school academic credentials. A college preparatory curriculum is expected. Three units each of college preparatory math and science are required, four years are highly recommended. Art, architectural, or technical drawing courses are highly recommended for applicants to Landscape Architecture. Advanced coursework at the honors or college level is highly recommended.
What the School Says...
SUNY-ESF offers these basic forms of student financial assistance: scholarships or grants; part-time employment; educational loans; diversity student scholarships and fellowships; assistantships, tuition scholarships, and fellowships for graduate students; a deferred tuition payment plan; and sources of non-need loans to students and parents.
What the School Says...
SUNY-ESF is unique among colleges and universities in that all of its educational and research programs are oriented toward natural resources and natural and designed environments. All that ESF is and does serves the mission of achieving a sustainable world. The college offers a full range of 24 undergraduate and 30 graduate degree programs covering the science, design, engineering, and management of the environment. It also provides students with a comprehensive understanding of environmental affairs. We bring together scientific, philosophical, theoretical, and practical perspectives on a range of environmental concerns. 
What the School Says...
SUNY-ESF is located in the city of Syracuse in the "University Hill" section overlooking downtown and Onondaga Lake. With a population of approximately 160,000 people, Syracuse is the largest city in Central New York and hosts an impressive array of cultural resources. 
What the School Says...
Graduates of this program have gone on to graduate programs in multiple disciplines, including law and medical school. They have also gone on to work in NGOs, education, government, and the private sector, pursuing careers in such areas as policy, advocacy, conservation, consulting, administration, law, and education.
