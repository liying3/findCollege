What the School Says...
Shaped by our vibrant urban setting and our Jesuit traditions, Loyola University Chicago offers you a unique educational environment. Our educational experience is transformative. It empowers you to develop your knowledge and skills, find your purpose, shape your identity, and make a difference in
your community. At Loyola, we offer you the tools you need to achieve success in the classroom, your career, your faith, your worldyour life. We represent and welcome all faiths and ethnic and economic backgrounds, with a common purpose of building a better society.
What the School Says...
For incoming freshman, a comprehensive review of your application materials includes your essay, counselor and teacher recommendations, official transcripts and standardized test scores (ACT or SAT), and an optional resume. The deadline for priority admission is December 1. To be considered for merit scholarships, you must submit an application by February 1. The application for admission is the application for merit scholarships. Students must be admitted by February 1 to be considered. 
What the School Says...
At Loyola, our Financial Aid Office is committed to guiding students and their families through the financial aid process. In addition to merit scholarships awarded at the time of admission, Loyola offers additional scholarships based on talent and financial need. Students may also qualify for outside scholarships, grants, loans, Federal Work-Study, or budget payment plans.
What the School Says...
Loyola is consistently ranked among the top universities in the country, and U.S. News & World Report recently named us one of the nation's best values in higher education. There are over 80 undergraduate majors to choose from within our 10 schools and colleges. With a 15:1 undergraduate student-to-faculty ratio, we ensure students get the attention they deserve and learn from an esteemed faculty, 97 percent of whom hold the highest academic degree in their field. Our programs will challenge you intellectually while allowing you to explore your talents, discover what inspires you, and help you succeed. You can also earn a dual degree, a double major, or a double minor, or complete a five-year joint bachelor's and master's degree program. 
What the School Says...
Among the many ways Loyola students can get involved, we offer nearly 200 clubs and organizations, more than 30 intramural and club sports, and over 12 men's and women's NCAA Division 1 athletic programs. Loyola is committed to your Loyola Experience, your life beyond Loyola, and the future of our shared environment. We recently introduced a new Institute of Environmental Sustainability, providing students practical experiences both inside and outside of the classroom that translate to your future workplace.  San Francisco Hall is a brand new residence hall that is LEED-certified with an alternative energy laboratory and very own greenhouse. 
What the School Says...
Learning in the Jesuit tradition is what distinguishes Loyola University Chicago (and its students) from many other colleges and is the foundation for your Loyola Experience. Loyola students are dedicated to and enthusiastic about their school and their experience here as demonstrated by the numerous notable accomplishments of current students as well as alumni. The camaraderie amongst classmates and friendly vibe are evident throughout Loyola's student community. One tour around any of our campuses and you will see that, at Loyola, you'll always belong.
