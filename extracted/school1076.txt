What the School Says...
CSU San Bernardino is a preeminent center of intellectual and cultural activity in Inland Southern California. Opened in 1965 and set at the foothills of the beautiful San Bernardino Mountains, the university serves more than 20,000 students each year and graduates about 4,000 students annually. CSUSB is listed among the best colleges and universities in the western United States, according to The Princeton Review, Forbes, and U.S. News and World Report, in their respective annual rankings. It also is part of the President's Higher Education Community Service Honor Roll for its commitment to service learning and civic engagement. 
What the School Says...
All CSU campuses use a combination of your high school college preparatory courses, grade point average, and SAT or ACT score to determine your eligibility for admission. We give priority to students who live within our defined local area. Students who graduate from high schools outside of this local area must meet additional admission standards and are admitted on a space-available basis. 
What the School Says...
Our Financial Aid Office administers a variety of programs to assist qualified students. Sources of student aid include federal Pell Grants, Perkins Loans, and work-study. A number of scholarships are awarded to new and continuing students, including our renewable President's Academic Excellence Scholarship for students who have ranked in the top 1 percent of their class, and the Mathematics and Science Scholars program, for top-performing local students who are interested in pursuing degrees in math and science fields.
What the School Says...
The university offers more than 70 bachelor's and master's degree programs, education credential and certificate programs, and a doctoral program. In addition to modern classrooms and laboratories, the university boasts distinctive features such as simulation laboratories, a modern instructional television studio lab, an electronic music studio, glass-blowing facilities, a Geographic Information Systems (GIS) Lab, a crime analysis lab, an anthropology museum, an infant/toddler teaching lab, the Arrowhead Lab for Security Analysis, the Watson and Associates Literacy Center, math and science teaching methods labs, and state-of-the-art computer laboratories.
What the School Says...
The diverse interests and lifestyles of our students are reflected in numerous student-run clubs and organizations as well as in activities sponsored by the university and the Associated Students. These programs include dances, drama productions, festivals, music concerts, guest lecturers, and small group socials. As for sports, CSUSB's Coyotes boast more than 100 Division II NCAA national championships. Plus, students may participate in recreational sports and outdoor activities through our Student Recreation and Fitness Center. Living on campus is one of the easiest ways to become involved at CSUSB. The Villages offers on-campus housing for students in three residential environments. Living on campus is a significant part of the CSUSB experience for approximately 1,500 students. 
What the School Says...
Reflecting the dynamic diversity of the region, CSUSB has the second highest African American and Hispanic enrollments of all public universities in California. Seventy percent of those who graduate are the first in their families to do so. CSUSB has placed student success at the forefront and strives to help students reach their greatest potential. Many services are available on the academic side to help students overcome any challenges they may face in the classroom. We also offer comprehensive career services to ensure that students experience a successful culmination of the educational experience.
