What the School Says...
Founded in 1859, The Cooper Union offers internationally recognized programs in architecture, art, and engineering. As one of the leading institutions in these fields, each school's curriculum uses uniquely challenging and innovative practices with world-renowned, award-winning faculty. The Cooper Union fosters excellent mentoring opportunities for students as they pursue their creative ambitions in a diverse setting. Because the campus is located in the heart of the East Village of New York City, students have access to an abundance of resources and can apply practical methods of learning in a dynamic and interactive setting.  




What the School Says...
The Cooper Union's individual schools each have separate requirements for admission. The School of Engineering seeks students with a high proficiency in math and science; The School of Art and the School of Architecture consider and review prospective students' portfolios. Space for admitted students is limited and competition for admission is high.


What the School Says...
All admitted students will receive a 50-percent-tuition scholarship for their entire course of study at the institution. For students who demonstrate greater need, the Office of Financial aid will determine additional funds up to 100 percent. The Cooper Union is committed to meeting the financial needs of students. 


What the School Says...
The Cooper Union's students are encouraged to pursue challenging academic goals using practical and interactive methods. All three schools rank among the top in their fields, and alumni are recognized internationally for their award-winning contributions in the professions of architecture, art, and engineering.




What the School Says...
With fewer than 1,000 students, The Cooper Union offers the intimacy of a small campus community in an exciting and dynamic location. The East Village, one of New York City's most diverse and energetic neighborhoods, offers an abundance of cultural activities, resources, and experiences, and many galleries, theaters, movie houses, restaurants, shops, and clubs.


What the School Says...
The Cooper Union's student body comprises exceptionally talented and unique students from across the globe. Its alumni network and international reputation offer students unique opportunities for competitive internships and jobs. The Cooper Union also facilitates opportunities for students to interact and collaborate, share their talents and interests, and utilize New York City as an extension of their classroom. Its various student organizations range from special interest groups to religious and performance groups, to several intercollegiate athletic teams, including men's and women's basketball, soccer, and tennis.
