What the School Says...
With all the brains of a nationally ranked research university and all the heart of a small college, SIU is where you want to be. You'll find the hands-on opportunities, top-ranked programs, and indispensable resources you need to develop intellectually and professionally. And a collaborative, supportive community that enables you to thrive. Plus, a serene wooded campus. Southern Illinois University in Carbondale is the quintessential college environment. It offers everything you need plus some things you never considered before. The experience forms around you.


What the School Says...
When determining the admissions decision of prospective students, SIU reviews students holistically.  In the holistic review we consider course rigor, grade point average, class rank, and standardized test scores, as well as a personal statement.  In the personal statement we are seeking applicants who are engaged through extracurricular activities, community service or have a commitment to employment.  Additionally, we are seeking to learn more about your academic potential, special talents, major life events, and your drive to succeed at a major research institution.


What the School Says...
At SIU, we've made it part of our mission to dismantle anything that stands between people and discovery. Whether through scholarships, grants, or loans, we put your college experience within reach.  Financial aid is available from federal, state, institutional and private sources.  Financial aid is available to students with financial need and those without financial need. At SIU we award more than eight million dollars in scholarships to undergraduate students annually and more than 1.7 million of the funding is awarded to new students.
We will guide you through the process and will be available to assist you every step along the way, ensuring that every person has the opportunity to succeed at SIU.



What the School Says...
We are a nationally ranked, top-tier research institution.  In every way, we've earned that distinction. Our faculty are published experts who are active in their fields. Our academic offerings extend from the classroom and the lab to your residence hall and overseas. Whatever your passion or interest, we offer more than 200 areas of undergraduate study, more than 70 graduate degrees, 30 doctoral degrees, a School of Law, and a School of Medicine.  Additionally, we boast an honors program, hands-on research opportunities for all students (undergraduate and graduate alike), study abroad opportunities in more than 300 sites globally, and living-learning communities for students who want to live with like-minded peers.  If you want to learn, we'll help take you as far as you want to go.


What the School Says...
SIU offers the quintessential college environment, and that includes life beyond the classroom.  You can feel the Saluki spirit all throughout campus.  It lives on in the passionate performances of our theatre productions. It energizes students in our rec center and intramural sports.  It inspires art, sparks debate, and reverberates in Carbondale and beyond.  We have countless ways to experience it.  With more than 400 student organizations, we offer opportunities to explore every dimension of your personality and interests.  We have it all: the marching band, intramural sports, religious affiliations, an active Greek community, academic affiliations, special interest groups, and much more.


What the School Says...
We are Salukis.  That's not just a name.  It's a belief that we're all capable of big things and we're all in this together.  It's a shared set of high expectationswhat we expect from ourselves as well as each other. It's a network of support in and out of the classroom. Each year, we welcome students from all 50 states and more than 100 countries who take advantage of outstanding academic programs as they pursue associate, bachelor's, master's, and  doctoral degrees, and professional degrees in law and medicine. Our alumni are more than 245,000 strong and reside in more than 100 countries across the world.  Once a Saluki, always a Saluki.
