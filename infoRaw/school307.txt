What the School Says...Discover a university that engages, challenges, and prepares you for a meaningful future. You'll find nationally recognized programs, innovative academics, and a dynamic mix of learning opportunities that transform you and your life. More and more students are discovering the advantages of a Bowling Green State University education.


What the School Says...The sooner you apply, the sooner you'll be able to start planning for the biggest adventure of your life. Apply to Bowling Green State University today, and get a head start on your future! The average ACT for entering freshmen is 22 and the average high school GPA is 3.23.


What the School Says...At Bowling Green State University, we keep the cost of higher education within reach. Low fees, financial aid, student employment opportunities, and payment plans combine to make the ultimate educational experience an affordable one. Currently, 70 percent of all BGSU students receive some type of financial assistance.


What the School Says...At Bowling Green State University, the future's wide open. With more than 200 undergraduate majors and programs of study in seven undergraduate colleges, as well as master's and doctoral level programs, BGSU offers an atmosphere that's charged with intellectual and creative energy. Many paths lead to ultimate success. Discover the one that's right for you.


What the School Says...Bowling Green State University is the home of over 20,000 students including nearly 6,000 students who live in the 15 residence halls and 31 houses on the Bowling Green campus. Additionally, our living and learning communities have earned national recognition by U.S. News & World Report for providing students with outstanding learning opportunities.


What the School Says...Within our transformative learning community, we build a welcoming, safe, and diverse environment where the creative ideas and achievements of all can benefit others throughout Ohio, the nation, and the world. Students come from 50 states and 70 other countries. Ethnic and racial minority students made up 22 percent of the entering fall 2011 class, the highest in BGSU's history.
