What the School Says...
The University of Wisconsin-Madison is a world-class university, nationally and internationally recognized for our academic excellence, incredible students, inspiring faculty, exceptional value, and an amazing campus and community. We invite you to learn more about UW-Madison and our academic programs, admission process, costs and financial aid, campus visits, and more.


What the School Says...
Admission to UW-Madison is holistic, competitive, and selective. In reviewing your application, we will consider your performance in rigorous coursework, written statements, standardized test scores, extracurricular activities, and an optional letter of recommendation. We are also looking for sustained involvement in activities in or out of school, leadership, community involvements, research, or any special gifts or talents that you would bring to our university. We're also seeking diversity in personal background and experience and your potential for contribution to the Wisconsin community.


What the School Says...
Our mission is to help you and your family with the cost of attending UW-Madison. The financial aid offered through our office includes grants, work-study, and loans. The funds for these programs come from the federal government, the State of Wisconsin, and the university. We offer money management and debt counseling and information on other kinds of financial aid, such as scholarships and student employment.


What the School Says...
UW-Madison offers instruction in more than 100 fields, with more than 4,200 course listings. Learning takes place in a variety of settings, from traditional classrooms, to undergraduate research opportunities, to service-learning experiences in the community. UW-Madison students are expected to apply learning inside and outside the traditional classroom in ways that have a positive impact on the world. Known as the Wisconsin Experience, this principle draws upon opportunities ranging from conducting research to embracing entrepreneurship to developing multicultural competency.




What the School Says...
Enjoy a beautiful campus comprised of 933 acres along the shore of Lake Mendota with five area lakes and 6,000 acres of parkland (including 260 parks and 14 conservation parks), and more than 150 miles of running, hiking, and biking trails in Madison. Select the University Residence Hall that fits you best and become part of a First-Year Interest Group. Choose from over 800 registered student organizations and unique residential learning communities on campus. Get the benefits of being in Wisconsin's capital city of Madison, which tops many "best" lists (best place to live,  best place to work) and is noted as one of America's safest cities and a top green city.


What the School Says...
UW-Madison is an incredible place to begin your career. You have countless opportunities to build your skill set and resum through your academic choices, student organization involvement, and other learning opportunities on campus. We have resources to help you research potential careers, look for a job or internship, apply to grad school, or improve your marketability.
