What the School Says...
Founded in 1864, the University of Denver offers a dynamic learning environment in the beautiful setting of the Rocky Mountain region. With a mid-sized student body of about 5,000 undergraduate students, an average class size of 20, and nearly 100 percent of courses being taught by professors, students at the University of Denver enjoy an active and engaging learning environment. Additionally, DU prides itself on offering extensive experiential learning opportunities. About 70 percent of students hold at least one internship throughout their undergraduate experience, many pursue independent research, and about 70 percent study abroad. Through the one-of-a-kind Cherrington Global Scholars program, DU students have the opportunity to study abroad at no additional cost, making an international experience both manageable and attainable. Additionally, with over 100 student organizations and the Rocky Mountains in its backyard, DU offers an active and engaging lifestyle for all of its students!



What the School Says...
While academic performance in high school plays a large role in admission decisions, the University of Denver takes a holistic approach in reviewing essays, letters of recommendation, SAT or ACT scores, and demonstration of personal qualities. Students who thrive at the University of Denver have strong character, a drive to achieve, and a dedication to improving society. 



What the School Says...
Nearly 84 percent of DU students receive some sort of financial assistance, and about 78 percent receive aid in the form of scholarships or grants. All students are automatically considered for annual merit awards at the point of admission. Students are also encouraged to apply for financial aid, using both the FAFSA and CSS PROFILE. 



What the School Says...
The University of Denver offers over 100 areas of undergraduate study, allowing students to pursue a unique array of academic interests. With an average class size of 20, DU students benefit from personalized instruction as well as diverse opportunities for scholarly inquiry, exploration, and critical thinking. Professors teach nearly 100 percent of courses; students learn from experts in their field of study. In and out of the classroom, DU students learn by doing, whether they're conducting independent research, studying abroad, or taking part in an internship. 



What the School Says...
DU students truly care about their academic achievement, but also remain active and involved both on campus and in the community. With a two-year requirement to live on campus, the University of Denver focuses on offering students a dynamic, engaging campus life experience. The University of Denver is also committed to being a "great private university dedicated to the public good," and students really take that to heart. Through many different student organizations and volunteerism, students are doing wonderful things both on campus and in the greater Denver area. 



What the School Says...
The University of Denver offers many resources to help students succeed, both in their classrooms and careers. The Anderson Academic Commons houses the Writing Center, Research Center, and Math Center, all of which provide free academic help and tutoring for students. DU also has a fantastic Career Center, which helps students with all aspects of internship and job placement. From resume building to mock interviews, career fairs to job searching, the Career Center aims to help students succeed in their undergraduate career as well as in their professional journey.
