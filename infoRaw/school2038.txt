What the School Says...
The beautiful University of Washington Bothell campus is located about 20 miles northeast of Seattle in a bio-tech and high-tech area with many global companies like Microsoft and Boeing. UW Bothell combines the benefits of a small college campus with the resources of a world-renowned university. Students experience small classes taught by world-class faculty while earning a prestigious University of Washington degree.


What the School Says...
UW Bothell holistically reviews all applications and operates on rolling admissions. Freshman applications are accepted for fall, winter, spring, and summer quarters. The freshman priority application and scholarship date for fall quarter is January 15. We require all freshman applicants and transfer applicants with less than 40 credits to submit official SAT or ACT test scores at the time of application. We use the best overall score from a single test date.


What the School Says...
Financial aid is available through scholarships, grants, loans, and work-study. Students must submit the FAFSA to be considered for financial aid. The UW Bothell FAFSA priority filing date is February 28. Aid is also available through the Husky Promise, which guarantees that UW tuition will be covered for qualified Washington state students from lower-income families. Students must submit the FAFSA by the priority filing date to be considered for Husky Promise funds.


What the School Says...
Our curriculum emphasizes close student-faculty interaction, collaboration amongst students, and hands-on learning with an interdisciplinary focus. Outstanding regional connections present students with unique opportunities for projects, internships, and research with leading businesses and organizations. At UW Bothell, students earn their degrees while building a solid foundation of relevant knowledge, practical skills, and professional preparation. Join the Husky family at UW Bothell and focus on your future today!



What the School Says...
The University of Washington Bothell offers students an active campus life, including a wealth of clubs, activities, and recreational and wellness programs. Our new Sports and Recreation Complex was designed to further enhance recreational opportunities. Student housing (Husky Village) at UW Bothell offers all the conveniences of apartment-style living coupled with residence life programming, a vibrant community of learners, and a safe environment to facilitate student success. In addition, students have many opportunities to pursue leadership positions and make a difference on campus. 


What the School Says...
As one student put it, "UWB is a culturally diverse institution driven by student initiatives. As students, our opinions are highly valued and supported by faulty and staff. We feel empowered to speak openly regarding campus environment, events, and policies. UWB prepares us for success by offering various leadership opportunities; promoting the use of learned skills beyond the classroom setting; and engaging in critical discussions."
